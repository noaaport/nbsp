
This is a reminder of the upcoming removal of a number of NEXRAD Level
III products from the NOAAPort SBN and the IDD.  (Recall that all NEXRAD
Level III products relayed in the IDD come from NOAAPort.)

NOAA Service Change Notice (SCN) 96 listed a number of NEXRAD Level
III products that were to be removed from NOAAPort around the end of
February, 2022.  Instead of these products actually being removed,
the TTAAII portion of their WMO IDs (which are included in the LDM/IDD
Product IDs) were changed to SDUS6i, and they continued to be distributed
in NOAAPort and in the IDD NEXRAD3 feed.

NOAA Service Change Notice 22-36, dated April 7, 2022:

announced that the SDUS6i products previously slated for removal from the
NOAAPort SBN will be removed on or about May 10, 2022 (one week from
tomorrow).

Here is the list of products currently being distributed in NOAAPort with

NB:

- removal of the N0Q product from NOAAPort will mean that the N0Q national 
  composite product that is distributed in the IDD FNEXRAD feed will
  stop being generated as NOAAPort is (currently) the only source of
  NEXRAD Level 3 products that are being distributed in the IDD

- Unidata has registered a request to get an LDM feed of all NEXRAD
  Level 3 products from the RPCCDS, this request has been approved by
  NOAA, but that feed source is not likely to become active for 2-3 months

  When this new source of level 3 products is up and running, we will
  resume creating the N0Q composite and distribute it in the IDD
  FNEXRAD feed.

- we are now creating a 1 km national composite from the N0B level 3
  product (so called Super Res Base Reflectivity) that was recently
  added to NOAAPort

  The IDD distribution of the N0B composite product will be activated
  at about 0 UTC on May 3 (NB: that is tonight at 6 pm MDT).
